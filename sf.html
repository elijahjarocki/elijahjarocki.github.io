San Francisco

city of hills

fold the people

strain for a landmark

of this crumpled blanket town

build her offices

and she will make murals

but ask her for murals

and she will ignore

evicted by efficiency

through coughing fits

obsessed with vanity

the cat curls its tail

the tourists will not understand your subtle loneliness

theyll all leave confused

but for the solitary wanderer

your streets are a map within

ive heard of your tantrums

the wrinkles run deep in your face

why cant i stand

flat

footed

on your chest

and why do your beaches swarm with flies?

hungry for the jewels of the colored lights

what rolls to the bottom of one hill

lies at the top of another

in the off beat rhythms

are you conducting a symphony?

where does your passion lie

when your parks are covered in haze and tar
